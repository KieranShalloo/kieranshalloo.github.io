Chromecast

The Chromecast experience is a small part of the overall All4 project that had not been received that well by the public due to lack of features. I was placed on this project to try and help push through a refreshed look and feel and to seize upon opportunities to improve the experience. With the UI being redesigned recently across platforms, this was also an opportunity to bring a fresh interpretation whilst removing user interactions from the TV screen

Autoplay

One main improvement was creating the ability for the Chromecast device to autoplay the next episode of a TV series. Although this was a common pattern in rival platforms it was missing from the all4 app. I explored the introduction of new UX patterns which felt familiar to the user whilst always remembering that the user couldn't interact directly with the TV and only through the mobile device. This in itself opened up many new challenges for the end user and also for working across both TV development teams and the Android team to ensure the work was synced up.

Figma Transition

I was part of the team that helped to restructure the design team away from using Sketch and moved towards figma for design and prototyping. With the change in the software also came a change in the approach of design system and the approach that the company wanted to take. By transitioning to Figma, Channel 4 also wanted to stop supporting the use of Abstract across the business. This led to a big change in the working relationship with developers and testers who had an established existing workflow. By moving our designs over to Figma, it gave us a chance to reevaluate the designs that we had in our files, the documentation we used and the handoff

Component Restructure

In the move to Figma, we used this as a reason to look deep into the files which we were using for our designs. We took this opportunity to fix any inconsistencies across the UI and as a way to reduce the number of components which we would be remaking. As Sketch components were built in a very different way to Figma, we introduced a Base Component system, with each component having a subset of variables which could be easily switched when needed.

Documentation

Documentation for developers was not one of the strongest points of the previous project. Designs were created in Sketch, and then handed off in Abstract but patterns were not documented clearly and were spread over different systems, some d in a wiki, some in Jira stories, some in miro and some not documented at all. As a design team, we came together to ensure that we had a consistent approach across all devices and platforms to ensure that developers did not have to battle to find designs or interactions

Organisation

With this strive for a more consistent approach, We had to ensure that we were aligned across the whole range of platforms on a sense of organisation. This included (but was not limited to) a concise set of naming conventions which aligned with developer naming conventions, a set structure for signing of final designs, and a creating a strong connection with the testing teams to ensure that work is at a high standard.

Roku Voice Search

For certification on the latest Roku devices, the updated Channel 4 app was missing a required feature to enable the release. This was using the Roku technology to enable voice search for the user. This was also on the Channel4 roadmap so it gave us an excuse to actually progress the work with an outside requirement.
