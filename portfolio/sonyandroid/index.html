Android TV Case Study

YouView On Sony TV

Brief

Help to bring the YouView system to Android TV by Google to allow it to be the default on all Sony televisions released in the UK. Adjust the user experience to fit in with Google guidleines and to work with the restricitons of the manufacturer

Client

YouView / Sony

Project Date

2017-2018

Role

Lead Product Designer

Becoming sony main tv interface

Bringing the Youview interface to the platform

The YouView platform was rewritten in HTML5 to allow it to be loaded onto the SONY TV and this allowed us the ability to upscale the interface to a native 1080p resolution, given the benefit of pixel-perfect icons and typography for the user. The change of technology also gave us the chance to use a component-based design system for the first time.

On screen Keyboard

As the platform was to be navigated using the SONY remote control, it affected the search section of the UI, leading us to create a bespoke On-screen keyboard exclusively for the YouView Sony project. We had to craft this feature from scratch as the existing user experience was based on text entry from the YouView remote, a feature which couldn’t be replicated with the sony control. As part of our design process, we shared the user testing results with the SONY team in Japan, helping to ensure that the feature would not interfere with the onboard google universal search

Set up Journeys

We needed to have an easy clear experience from the users first interaction with the new tv service. By integrating with the google setup journey the benefits for the user enabling the features could be explained at the very first moment

Integration

We were able to bring the youview benefits to the user with the project, highlighting the relationship YouView already had with the major UK channels, allowing their content to be deeply engrained in the SONY ecosystem, available at the touch of a button for people browsing for new content.

Entry Points

With a service such as Android tv, the user has the ability to update and replace apps with their own choices. This could lead to a case where the YouView features are hidden from the user who in turn may struggle to find them again. Part of the requirements allowed us to have a selection of key access points for the user, including; the GUIDE & SEARCH key’s on the remote, a tile on the android home screen as well as sections on SONY’s customisable rail carousel

Android Home Screen

The Android home screen is the central point for the whole of the android TV interface. It is the first and main point of interaction for the user. We needed to utilize the space we were allowed here, by creating a strong interactive element, as well as creating flows which gave the maximum reach for the user to find the youview interface

Concepts & Feedback

We presented our work to both senior members of our team as well as external stakeholders on a regular basis. Using a agile review process it gave the teams abroad a way to be involved in the design process and it gave the design team a set goal to accomplish. Taking feedback was a tricky process as the separate companies all had their own review process which led to a lot of time waiting for admin to pass through back into useful design feedback. A company as large as Sony has a very different process which consisted of various people and teams syncing up before answers could be given.

Subtle Design Touches

There were a few sections which required reworking and alterations due to the impact of delivery times with the project. We looked to create bespoke versions of existing patterns that our customers had become accustomed too. We create a set of design components which corresponded with the development team and the google established guidelines

Delivered Designs

The final finished design of the the project brought together feedback from stakeholders in the project into an approved visual and interaction design model. The final designs and documentation was then sent over to the Sony Tokyo office where it was integrated into the final packaged TV
