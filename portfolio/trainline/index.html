Trainline

Engaging the users

Brief

Brought into the project originally as a short cover, I helped the team to progress their drive towards a engaging platform turning users into some thing

Client

Trainline

Project Date

2024

Role

Senior UX/UI Designer (contractor)

CMS templates

Trainline has multiple areas on its platform where promotional content can be placed. To save development time these sections are controlled by a CMS tool. The issue with this, was a lack of a template system, with multiple sizes and styles of sections with no solid theme across them. I took control of this as the team i worked with controlled the CMS tool, crafting documentation and guides for engineering to create a uniform visual and interactive style to ensure users can all navigate through the platforms.

Marketing

Push to app

Trainline wanted to promote their app to users to move from the mobile version of the website. With this in mind, we introduced certain promotional messages to the user to highlight the benefits they would receive if they used the app.

We wanted to ensure that we were promoting the content to the people who wanted to use the app, especially as the data told us there was a strong feeling across mainland europe where users prefer to use mobile websites rather than apps .

Marketing preferences

It was key to enable the users to be able to control their marketing information. When i arrived at Trainline, this was wrapped up in enabling the user to see vital user journey.



Working with multiple internal teams we created a proof of concept to split out the marketing channel messages from the journey information. Although this started as a simple idea, it escalated with information having to communicate through different databases spending on where the user signed up and which platform they chose their options on

Marketing Opt in / Opt out

For different user scenarios, based on user marketing preferences, we wanted to add in a few alternative approaches to allow the user to Opt-in or Opt-out to relevant marking messages. We had to incorporate a wide array of scenarios here, as users could have chosen their marketing preferences on a different device that they were now on and not all of the Trainline databases shared this information with each other.

We also had scenarios where users had previously purchases tickets as a ‘guest’ account and we had to investigate how those choices could be brought into their account as the tickets were also brought over.


For these scenarios we put a subset of users into multivariate testing to see which option worked best, hitting Trainline targets with no diminishing returns for the end user

Creating a vision

With these smaller pieces of content being developed, The team was missing a strong future vision. Working with a collection of Trainline employees i helped to craft a long term strategy piece which would create interest in the platform with the goal of rewarding users who remain customers year in, year out.

We decided to focus on the problem that Traibnline currently treated their userbase exactly the dame on day 1 on the platform as they did on day 1000. We wanted to show how trainline could reward the users who came back

With our problem statement agreed, I went away and worked on some high level conceptual pieces which could in turn form an overall long-term focus ready to be split into manageable product stories and projects.

Once these were presented back we had positive feedback on three ideas we were recommended to take forward, These were….

Loyalty Scheme

Here we crafted a way to reward users. We had two different approaches which we designed and took into user testing.

The first approach was to give the user points as a reward system. This could then unlock rewards to further the users experience given them access to unique awards and limited availability discounts.

As this was all conceptual pieces of work we could really push the interaction design and utilise patterns and logic that didn’t currently exist on the platforms. Using figma to create prototypes allowed us to create fast turnaround for usability testing to really get user feedback on our overall concept pieces

Railcard recommendation

Users had commented via social media platforms that when pourchasing tickets, trainline wasn’t giving clear communication on ways the user could save money when they could purchase a railcard.

This one off payment could save money for a user making journeys regularly and was an easy way to encourage users to stay on the platform post journey.

My hypothesis was by ensuring we the users side, even with a loss on the journey this could help transfer users from a one time purchase to a reoccurring customer.

Savings accumulation

The first piece of work was created to enable users to see the benefits of Trainline as they became more frequent users. I conceived up ways to show the user how much money they had saved by using Trainline, highlighting unique features that the user couldn't get anywhere else such as SplitSave (a way Trainline splits tickets for a cheaper fare) as well as CO2 savings and time savings compared to travelling by car.

This in turn would co-exist with the company's existing sustainability practises and would long term give the user a full view of their savings beyond exclusively cost.

There were various aspects of the design that we iterated the phone. The initial design started with an interactive graph, which users would instinctively be able to see how their savings have grown over the last 30 days 90 days or six months.

This in tern was connected to an overall number at the top of the screen, which would been updated with a growing accumulated savings since the user had been a trainline member This graph also became a great way to also upsell of the savings accumulated jour instance an easy way for the user to jump into the sustainability story section of the app to see how much CO2 they had saved before we could integrate the long-term vision without removing the MVP speed of build and iteration .

When user are testing we also looked at giving the user the ability to see exactly where they had saved their money. This could have been done between individual journeys individual journey times or destinations during user testing. This didn’t have as much positive response as I initially thought uses saying that although it’s quite beneficial, it didn’t really add to the oval experience so this was and then the user would do this much epoured from the MVP and Was put in the backlog to work on and design further
