Trainline

Engaging the users

Brief

Brought into the project originally as a short cover, I helped the team to progress their drive towards a engaging platform turning users into some thing

Client

Trainline

Project Date

2024

Role

Senior UX/UI Designer (contractor)

CMS templates

Trainline has multiple areas on its platform where promotional content can be placed. To save development time these sections are controlled by a CMS tool. The issue with this, was a lack of a template system, with multiple sizes and styles of sections with no solid theme across them. I took control of this as the team i worked with controlled the CMS tool, crafting documentation and guides for engineering to create a uniform visual and interactive style to ensure users can all navigate through the platforms.

Approaching the problem

With a view to get this feature live as fast as possible we had a 5 day turn around to go from a concept to a design that was ready for development. I took the initiative to bring some elements of existing designs and concepts already in the design system to bring this to life. With one eye on the developer resource needed for this project it was all about quick wins and a scalable concept we could iterate on top off!

Marketing

Helping users opt in

The quick win for this project would be to reuse some design system principles but to adjust them for the user coming from the Google Trains platform. This, for me, meant that we should value the journey the user has made to come to the trainline above everything else.

I first brought the selected train journey to the top of the page. Clear messaging is to be applied to the selected option to make it stand out as clearly as possible. Underneath a reassurance message will be placed in a easy to access position giving the user the benefits of using the Trainline platform.

This clickable element will open a new modal which will be able to sell personalised benefits of the platform based on common highly rated features with positive feedback.

Creating a vision

With these smaller pieces of content being developed, The team was missing a strong future vision.

Working with a collection of Trainline employees i helped to craft a long term strategy piece which would create interest in the platform with the goal of rewarding users who remain customers year in, year out.

We decided to focus on the problem that Traibnline currently treated their userbase exactly the dame on day 1 on the platform as they did on day 1000.

We wanted to show how trainline could reward the users who came back

With our problem statement agreed, I went away and worked on some high level conceptual pieces which could in turn form an overall long term focus ready to be split into manageable product storys and projects.

Once these were presented back we had positive feedback on three ideas we were recommended to take forward, These were….

Loyalty Scheme

Savings accumulation

The first piece of work was created to enable users to see the benefits of Trainline as they became more frequent users. I conceived up ways to show the user how much money they had saved by using Trainline, highlighting unique features that the user couldn't get anywhere else such as SplitSave (a way Trainline splits tickets for a cheaper fare) as well as CO2 savings and time savings compared to travelling by car. This in turn would co-exist with the company's existing sustainability practises and would long term give the user a full view of their savings beyond exclusively cost.
