Visual Scrubbing

The Altogether Different streaming service

Brief

I worked with Channel4 throughout a variety of projects, bringing my TV experience to their design team as an external contractor.

I was able to bring an outside perspective to the problems of the business whilst working with the internal design teams as well as a third-party technology supplier.

One example of this was Visual Scrubbing

Client

Channel 4

Project Date

2023

Role

Senior UX/UI Designer (contractor)

Visual Scrubbing

Channel 4 app was limited in its ability to rewind and fast forward, with customer feedback saying this was especially noticeable when the user was dropped into the incorrect placement of an episode on the continue watching area

Conceptually thinking

We knew we wanted to create a visual style that would fit in with the current on screen designs that users have been exposed to over the last few years. We didnt want this feature to feel too different to the experience so we approach the design elements with a clear userbase in mind.

There were a few different concepts we had been looking at for these conceptual pieces. By looking at competitors in research we narrowed down our thought process to three different interaction patterns and put them in front of users.

The Design Process

There were a few different concepts we had been looking at for these conceptual pieces. By looking at competitors in research we narrowed down our thought process to three different interaction patterns and put them in front of users.

5 tiles Version

The first concept brough 5 tiles to the user as they were interacting with the scrubbing element of the screen. We had alot of positive feedback but also came to a few issues in relation to where the focus sat in the screen in relation to playback. It felt that a central navigation fought with the playback area when a user wasnt in the rough middle of the programme. If they were at the beginning or end of the show the expectation was a tile around the area of the progress in the show

Pros

Users could see more of the show they were watching
Easier to recognise parts of a show you had already watched
More tiles made content easier to skim

Cons

Possible spoilers
Lots of movement could cause accessibility issues
Expensive tech resource

3 tiles Version

We also looked at a middle ground of three tiles to reduce the amount of content on screen from the 5 tiles design but give more context to where the user was in the programme. With more than one tile on screen we did have to start thinking about moments that could spoil a show for a user. Maybe a contenstand was voted off the show before they had got there and they saw on a tile

1 tile Version

This led into using a one tile design which would follow the users playhead throughout the prgramme. This could be invoked atr the precise area the user was currently looking when they decided they wanted to either rewind or fast forward. .

Advert Breaks

Advert breaks were a big element of the design in visual scrubbing. Certain questions were raised around feedback for the user when crossing advertisement breaks. Would the scrubbing stop to play the advert? How would the user know an advert break was going to be invoked after playback is started?.

Desiging for Roku

Although we had our prefered design on the product, Roku had a version of visual scrubbing built into the product already.

This led to discussions about how we would preocede on Roku. was the right approach to keep consistency with the rest of the designs in tv platforms, or to create less friction to release to the public. After some high level consultation it was decided to make the development team life easier by using the built in features.

This reduced resource needed as well as rallowing roku users to have a familiar pattern that they would have used on different platforms on thier device..
